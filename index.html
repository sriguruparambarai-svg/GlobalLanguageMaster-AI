Pronunciation Tips
Common pronunciation rules for English

🔸 Silent letters: knife (k is silent), write (w is silent), hour (h is silent)

🔸 -tion ending: Always sounds like "shun" — nation, station, education

🔸 -ed ending: walked = walkt, played = playd, wanted = wantid

🔸 Stress: PHOtograph (noun) vs phoTOgraphy (verb/adj)

🔸 Vowel sounds: "a" in cat vs cake vs ball — all different!

Communication Skills
Learn to speak confidently — interviews, public speaking, polite conversation
🤝 Polite Conversation
  • ✅Always say Please, Thank you, Excuse me, Sorry
  • ✅Make eye contact when speaking
  • ✅Listen without interrupting
  • ✅Use "Could you please...?" instead of "Give me..."
  • ✅Greet people properly: "Good morning", "How are you?"
Student A: Good morning! Could you please tell me where the library is?
Student B: Good morning! Of course, it's on the second floor, near the stairs.
Student A: Thank you very much! That's very helpful.
Student B: You're welcome. Have a nice day!
🎤 Interview Skills
  • 🔹Introduce yourself clearly: "Good morning. My name is... I am studying in class..."
  • 🔹Speak slowly and clearly — don't rush
  • 🔹Answer in complete sentences, not just yes/no
  • 🔹If you don't understand, say: "Could you please repeat the question?"
  • 🔹End with: "Thank you for this opportunity"
🏆 Public Speaking Tips
  • ⭐Start with a strong opening — a question, story or fact
  • ⭐Speak to the audience, not to the board
  • ⭐Use pauses for effect — silence is powerful
  • ⭐Vary your voice — loud for important points, soft for stories
  • ⭐End with a clear conclusion: "In conclusion..."
